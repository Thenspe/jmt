What is Music Therapy?

Music Therapy is a scientifically proven and allied health profession. Musical elements and interventions such as rhythm and melody are skillfully and purposefully used by a Certified Music Therapist to address social, emotional, cognitive and, physical health. A holistic approach is used to build a therapeutic relationship enhancing the well-being and needs of the individual and to facilitate desired outcomes such as learning, self-expression, and personal growth.

Music Therapist

In order to practice music therapy, one needs to become a Certified Music Therapist (Credentials: MTA). This entails a four-year university bachelor degree, completing a 1,000- hour supervised clinical internship and passing a recognized certified exam which tests the knowledge of musical skills, therapy approaches, practices and techniques. Upon passing the exam the application and certification is signed through the Canadian Association of Music Therapists (CAMT). Continuing education and following the Code of Ethics is mandatory to stay in good standing as an MTA.

Music Therapy Process

Music Therapy sessions are designed on individualized long-term and short-term wellness goals.

  1. Referral or intake form - An intake helps the therapist learn about the client before the first therapy session takes place. It also helps the therapist to develop interventions that are best for the client.
  2. Assessment - an initial assessment can take 2-4 sessions. In these sessions the music therapist will learn more about the client to establish a baseline.

    3. Treatment Plan - If available, the music therapist will collaborate with support team (POA, guardians, parents, staff, other disciplines) to individualize the objectives and goals for the client. During treatment musical interventions such as singing, songwriting, improvisation, listening, lyrical analysis, breath work etc.., may be used to address goals.

  3. Review - After a recommended period, the music therapist will review and reassess to determine if goals and objectives need to be modified or have been achieved. Once goals have been achieved a recommendation of being discharged will be discussed.

Who can benefit?

Music Therapy can be provided in either a group or individual setting. Musical training or experience is NOT required in order to engage in sessions. Music therapy is accessible to individual of all ages, abilities and diverse needs, including but not limited to:

  • Children, Youth, and Adults on the Autism spectrum
  • Intellectual developmental disabilities and physical disabilities
  • Older adults living with dementia or Alzheimer’s
  • Children with attention and learning challenges
  • Veterans
  • Anxiety
  • Rehabilitation (Stroke, Parkinson’s, Aphasia etc..)
  • Neonatal intensive Care
  • Pain Management
  • Depression
  • Speech and language impairments

Benefits

Increases Communication- Music encourages meaningful social interaction within a group or with the therapist.

Pain management- music can ease pain. It affects our hormones (increase dopamine and oxytocin), which can create a distraction, resulting in relaxation and lowering anxiety levels.

Music Therapy is a therapeutic profession which focuses on well-defined objective and attainable goals for each individual. Music Therapists use their knowledge of the power music to integrate towards the some of the following goals: communication, expression, self-regulation, elevation in mood, relaxation, focus, motor, and social interaction.
